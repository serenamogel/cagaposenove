Garlic Bread in Air Fryer

Garlic bread is one of those side dishes that just seems to go with everything. Grilling up some chicken? Make up some garlic bread. Pasta night? That’s gonna need some garlic bread. Making up a pot of soup? How about some garlic bread?

Most nights that I make garlic bread, it tends to be the star of the meal with everyone fighting over the last piece. Making garlic bread in the air fryer makes quick work of this easy side dish and it’s a total crowd pleaser. It’s perfectly crispy on the edges with a soft center and tons of garlic butter for the most amazing flavor.

What To Serve With Air Fryer Garlic Bread

There is no wrong way to serve this easy side dish but here are a few of our very favorite recipes that we enjoy with this tasty bread:

  • Stuffed Shells
  • Chicken Alfredo Bake
  • Lazy Day Lasagna
  • Gumbo
  • Zuppa Toscana
  • Crockpot Chili

    • French baguette – or sourdough if you love that.

    Garnish

    • finely chopped fresh parsley

    How To Make Garlic Bread in Air Fryer

  • Preheat air fryer to 350°F.
  • Mix together butter, minced garlic, Parmesan cheese and parsley.
  • Cut the baguette in half and then in half again lengthwise.
  • Spread garlic butter on each half and then cut each half into two for a total of 8 pieces.
  • Cook 5 to 7 minutes or until the desired crispiness has been reached. Repeat with the remaining half baguette.
  • Garnish with fresh parsley and serve immediately.

    • Cheesy Garlic Bread

    Feeling cheesy? Mozzarella cheese is an amazing and tasty addition to this recipe. Add shredded mozzarella to the top of the garlic bread slices when there is about one minute left of cooking time in the air fryer.

    Storage Information

    Refrigerator: Leftover garlic bread can be store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.

    Freezer: Let garlic bread cool, then transfer to a freezer-safe, airtight container. Good for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge or on the counter before reheating in the air fryer, oven or microwave.

    Trish’s Tips and Tricks

    Check out my best tips for the ultimate air fryer garlic bread:

    • Be sure to slice your baguette halves to the same thickness so that the bread all toasts evenly.
    • If you like a really garlicky garlic bread you may want to add another clove of garlic. If you don’t like fresh garlic you can substitute garlic powder to taste.
    • The Parmesan adds some extra flavor to this bread but feel free to skip it if you prefer.
    • Remove garlic bread from the air fryer right when cooking is complete to avoid it overcooking and getting hard. If you want to serve the entire baguette at once, wrap the cooked garlic bread in foil and place it in a 200 degree oven or toaster oven until the second batch is ready.
    • Leftover garlic bread can be reheated for 30 seconds in the microwave or air fryer but it really is best eaten fresh.

    All air fryers cook differently. I used the Cosori Air fryer for this recipe. You may have to adjust time and temperature if you have a different model.

    Air Fryer Garlic Bread

    This Air Fryer Garlic Bread is made with homemade garlic butter and ready to go in about 10 minutes. Perfectly crispy and decadently buttery with just the right amount of garlic! Bet you can’t stop at just one slice!

    Course bread, Side Dish Cuisine American Keyword air fryer garlic bread, garlic bread air fryer, garlic bread in air fryer Prep Time 5 minutes minutes Cook Time 5 minutes minutes Servings 8 Calories 199kcal Author Trish – Mom On Timeout

    Ingredients

    • ½ cup salted butter softened
    • 1 clove garlic minced or ½ teaspoon of garlic powder
    • ¼ cup Parmesan cheese freshly grated
    • 1 teaspoon dried parsley or 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
    • 1 french baguette

    Garnish

    • finely chopped fresh parsley

    Instructions

    • Preheat air fryer to 350°F.

    • In a small bowl mix together the softened butter, minced garlic, Parmesan cheese and parsley until combined.

    • Cut the baguette in half and then in half again lengthwise. Spread each half generously with the garlic butter. Then cut each half into two for a total of 8 pieces.

    • When the air fryer is hot, arrange the bread in a single layer on the tray. Cook 5 to 7 minutes or until the desired crispiness has been reached. Repeat with the remaining half baguette.

    • Garnish with fresh parsley and serve immediately.

    Notes

    Storage Information Refrigerator: Leftover garlic bread can be store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Freezer: Let garlic bread cool, then transfer to a freezer-safe, airtight container. Good for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge or on the counter before reheating in the air fryer, oven or microwave.
