Ive Gone Through 4+ Tubes of This Brow Code Gel

I’ve laminated, waxed, shaved, tinted, and bleached my brows. Fun as my dabblings have been, I always return to a fluffy, feathery style, à la Brooke Shields; in my pursuit of full, shapely arches, I’ve brushed on a billion brow gels. Of the few I’ve kept around through the years, a fairly under-the-radar product, the Brow Code Tinted Multi-Peptide Brow Gel, has proven incomparable in its ability to deliver instantly sculpted, compliment-worthy arches. 

Perhaps the most memorable compliment came from celebrity makeup artist extraordinaire Katie Jane Hughes, who once inquired about what I’d used on my arches earlier this year. “It looks like it has really good hold,” she’d noted. Naturally, her assessment was spot-on; the formula does, indeed, boast superior hold, albeit without stiffness or flaking inherent to many stronghold brow products. 

Created by a celebrity brow artist, the Brow Code Tinted Multi-Peptide Brow Gel is a gel-meets-pomade formula that shapes, lifts, defines, and tints arches to their boldest and most polished-looking state. Furthermore, the formula features ingredients that can help “support hair growth” over time, per the brand. Available in four natural-looking hues, it adds fullness, volume, and rich, pigmented color that resists smudging or flaking throughout the day. 

A single swipe of the spoolie-style applicator deposits even color, and leaves my brows looking as if I’d just had them professionally dyed and laminated. It takes mere seconds to create enviable arches, and the results last all day. Case in point: It’s about 8 PM, and while the rest of my makeup could certainly use a touchup, my brows look freshly done.  

In addition to the instant aesthetic gratification this tinted gel provides, it also delivers nourishment over time, which can support healthier, shinier brows, thanks to an infusion of five growth-promoting peptides. Additionally, the formula is spiked with hydrating hyaluronic acid, softening vitamin B5, and biotin, all of which have been shown, in one way or another, to boost hair health.

Shoppers echo my sentiments for this under-the-radar must-have. According to one, “It lasts all day long, it doesn’t smudge or flake,” and leaves “eyebrow hair feel[ing] hydrated,” even after removal. Another reviewer cites “full and fluffy” arches, while another notes that the single-swipe spoolie makes their “brow routine so quick and easy.” I couldn’t agree more. 

For your fullest, fluffiest brows, shop the Brow Code Tinted Multi-Peptide Brow Gel for $40. If supermodel-style arches are your aspiration, it’s likely the last brow gel you’ll ever use.
